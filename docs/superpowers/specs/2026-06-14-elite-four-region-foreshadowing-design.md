# Elite Four Region Foreshadowing Design

## Intent

The Kanto Elite Four should still play like the classic endgame gauntlet, but narratively it should stop feeling like the end of the whole game. This slice makes each Elite Four member a controlled preview of the wider World Circuit without unlocking travel before Champion Blue is defeated.

## Proposed Story Beat

- Lorelei tests cold patience and hints that island regions can hide dangerous pressure beneath warm seas, tying her to later Alola and ice-island themes.
- Bruno frames strength as tradition and discipline, pointing toward Johto's towers, vows, and older ways of training.
- Agatha confirms that Team Moonlight's grief signal is larger than Lavender Tower and warns Antman not to let grief borrow a friend's face.
- Lance turns dragons into the first explicit Blackthorn and World Circuit warning. Blue is Champion now, and the World Circuit will not open for second place.

## Required Player Experience

- Battle flow, trainer IDs, flags, doors, and room progression remain unchanged.
- Each Elite Four intro/post-battle text adds a future-region signal.
- WorldLink logs the Elite Four as regional anchors after Indigo begins.
- Red remains outside and silent as a respectful companion choice.
- Johto remains locked until Champion Blue is defeated and Oak issues the passport.

## Scope Boundary

This slice does not rebalance Elite Four teams, alter Champion Blue, issue the World Circuit Passport, or unlock Johto. Those are separate Act 7 slices.
