# Victory Road Rival Standings Design

## Intent

This slice turns the Earth Badge payoff into a real pre-League climb. Route 22 Blue remains the classic late rival battle, but the scene now carries the emotional pressure built across Kanto: Red is Antman's friend, Misty and Brock are active companions, Ava and Dax are live rivals, and Blue feels himself being written out of the story unless he wins at Indigo.

## Proposed Story Beat

Blue confronts Antman before Victory Road. He is not joking; he is trying to prove that his solo road is stronger than Antman's companion-supported road. Red waits at the gate but does not enter the fight. Misty and Brock send League prep notes through WorldLink. Victory Road then becomes a standings gauntlet: Blue is ahead, Ava mapped safe switch routes, Dax left training marks, and WorldLink pauses cave feed so the player feels isolated inside the final Kanto test.

Indigo Pokemon Center confirms the rule: Red, Misty, and Brock can support Antman outside, but the Kanto League does not allow partners. Johto remains locked until the Champion record clears and Oak can issue the World Circuit Passport.

## Required Player Experience

- Route 22 Blue has the final Kanto pre-League pressure battle.
- Blue explicitly frames Indigo as the place he passes Antman and Red.
- Victory Road tells the player that rival progress is still happening, but cave/dungeon notification noise is paused.
- Red chooses not to carry Antman through Victory Road.
- Misty and Brock support with prep, not battle access.
- Indigo explains the World Circuit Passport without unlocking Johto before Champion clearance.

## Scope Boundary

This slice does not change Elite Four parties, champion scripts, Sky Pass, rematches, or Johto travel. Those belong in later Act 7 slices.
