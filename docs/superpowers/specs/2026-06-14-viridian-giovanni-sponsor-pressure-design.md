# Viridian Giovanni Sponsor Pressure Design

## Intent

Viridian must still feel like the classic eighth Gym: Antman reaches the locked final Kanto badge, discovers Giovanni is the Leader, beats him, earns the Earth Badge, and receives TM26. The twist is that Giovanni is no longer only the mastermind. By this point he has realized a sponsor used Rocket's ambition, Silph pressure, Phoenix science, Gold Dust markets, and Meridian Gate language as pieces of a larger region-control network.

## Proposed Story Beat

Giovanni returns to Viridian because the Gym is the last legal Kanto gate he can control. He challenges Antman as both Gym Leader and Rocket kingpin, but his dialogue leaks resentment toward an unnamed sponsor. This keeps the Kanto story readable, preserves Giovanni's pride, and withholds the full Nexus Order reveal for a later region.

The Earth Badge WorldLink handoff opens Victory Road only. Johto remains locked until Indigo clearance so WorldLink stays a true journey rather than a quick jump system.

## Required Player Experience

- The classic eighth Gym battle remains mandatory and recognizable.
- Giovanni says the Gym is no longer a hideout; it is the final Kanto gate.
- Giovanni names the sponsor as a manipulator but does not reveal the final faction name.
- The Meridian Gate is referenced as something Giovanni did not fully own.
- Earth Badge handoff verifies Kanto badge completion, opens Victory Road, and explicitly says Johto remains locked.
- Support NPCs imply Rocket subordinates noticed outside pressure.
- Red, Misty, Brock, Blue, Ava, Dax, and Lyra all get WorldLink/rival progression hooks for the pre-League transition.

## Scope Boundary

This slice does not implement Sky Pass local Fly, Kanto rematches, new Blue battles, or Johto travel. Those are separate slices after the Viridian/Indigo bridge is stable.
