# Pokemon Nexus Red - Red Companion System

Date: 2026-06-13

## 1. Role

Red is Antman's friend from Pallet Town and the living connection to the original Pokemon Red path. He is not the protagonist. He is the trainer Antman grew up hearing about, then actually gets to travel with across the full game.

Red should feel special without stealing the game. He is present often enough to feel like a real travel companion, not a rare cameo.

Design rules:

- Red helps at key moments, but Antman solves the journey.
- Red can follow Antman through routes, towns, and dungeons.
- Red can join tag battles and training battles.
- Red is AI-controlled in tag battles.
- Red can give tactical advice and route observations.
- Red cannot fight Antman's gym battles.
- Red cannot clear major solo trials for Antman.
- Red should step back before boss moments that define Antman's growth.

## 2. Early Kanto Use

Red appears before Oak's Lab or shortly after the starter scene.

Opening idea:

- Antman exits home.
- Red is waiting near the Pallet grass line, watching unusual Pokemon movement.
- Red says little but confirms Oak is worried.
- Blue is irritated because Red is paying attention to Antman.
- Red does not take a starter from Oak; he already has Pikachu or a compact early team.

## 3. Companion States

Red should not be glued to the player every second, but he should recur through the entire game. Use companion states:

- present_in_town: Red appears for dialogue or a scene.
- follows_temporarily: Red walks with player through a short route/dungeon.
- follows_region_segment: Red travels with Antman through a longer story segment.
- tag_partner: Red joins a scripted double battle.
- training_partner: Red offers controlled practice battles or team-building advice.
- worldlink_contact: Red sends a short message.
- absent_investigating: Red is offscreen advancing a parallel lead.
- superboss_available: postgame friend battle unlocked.

## 4. Kanto Companion Beats

### Pallet Town

Red notices the route migration before Oak explains it. He tells Antman, "Do not ignore small changes. They become big ones."

### Route 1

Optional short follow section. Red points out that a Johto starter should not be nesting near Pallet.

### Viridian Forest

Red appears if the player struggles or after a rival update. He does not solve the dungeon; he marks a hidden item or warns about a Rocket scout.

### Pewter Museum

Red can be the tag partner if Ava or Dax is not selected. His presence makes Rocket panic.

### Post-Brock

Red leaves to check Route 22 and Mt. Moon rumors, creating space for Antman to lead.

### Cerulean/Vermilion

Red returns as a short travel companion after Misty, helping Antman process that friends can join the journey without reducing the rivalry.

### Lavender

Red joins briefly because the dream disturbance feels dangerous. He supports Antman in a tag battle or protection scene, then lets Antman resolve the Tower's key conflict.

### Cinnabar

Red speaks more than usual about power, restraint, and why some Pokemon mysteries should not be treated like trophies.

## 5. Red Team Philosophy

Red's team should grow slowly and be used sparingly.

Early:

- Pikachu
- Bulbasaur/Charmander/Squirtle rotating cameo only if needed
- Snorlax or Lapras should not appear early

Midgame:

- Pikachu
- Kanto ace rotation
- one Pokemon that reflects the current region

Late game:

- iconic Red-style full team,
- no gimmick spam unless postgame,
- extremely strong but fair.

## 6. Mechanical Uses

Red can enable:

- optional double battle tutorials,
- tag battle spectacle,
- emergency story rescue once only,
- lore contrast with Blue,
- postgame friend battle,
- WorldLink "Red's Notes" tips.
- optional sparring battles that teach battle mechanics.
- route companion banter.
- emotional continuity between regions.

Red should not:

- heal the player constantly,
- block player choice,
- defeat bosses for the player,
- appear in every town without purpose,
- become an eleventh rival feed spammer.

## 8. Full-Game Regional Presence

Red should appear in every region with a defined purpose:

- Kanto: friend, mentor, early companion.
- Johto: investigates Mt. Silver/Rocket echoes and joins one Rocket/Moonlight segment.
- Hoenn: helps during a weather crisis route, then sends field notes.
- Sinnoh/Hisui: reacts strongly to space-time danger and trains Antman before Mt. Coronet.
- Unova: becomes the quiet counterpoint to N's philosophy.
- Kalos: observes Mega Evolution and questions power used for image.
- Alola: joins one island/wormhole segment and protects civilians during an Ultra Beast event.
- Galar: appears in a stadium exhibition/training scene, but does not enter Antman's gym battles.
- Paldea: helps at the Academy/Area Zero threshold, then follows Antman's lead.
- World Nexus Championship: tag partner in a crisis battle and optional postgame friend battle.

## 7. Relationship With Blue

Blue resents that Red treats Antman like a peer. This gives Blue a cleaner emotional arc:

- early jealousy,
- midgame rivalry escalation,
- late respect,
- World Championship acceptance.

Blue should eventually admit that chasing Red made him miss what Antman was becoming.

## 8. Relationship With Antman

Antman starts as the new trainer following Red's path. By the final act, Red is following Antman's lead into the Nexus crisis.

Key emotional turn:

During the World Nexus Championship, Red says or implies: "This one is your road."
