# Pokemon Nexus Red - Nine Region Design Framework

Default protagonist: Antman, a new Pallet Town trainer following Red's path

## 1. High-Level Pitch

Pokemon Nexus Red is a full-world Pokemon saga that begins like Pokemon Red and grows into a connected journey across Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh/Hisui, Unova, Kalos, Alola, Galar, and Paldea. The player starts in Pallet Town as Antman, a new trainer following Red's path with Red as an early friend and occasional travel companion, and discovers that the strange appearance of faraway starter Pokemon on Kanto's first routes is not a convenience but the first symptom of a global crisis. Ten rivals travel the same circuit, villain teams collide across regions, Team Rocket becomes the recurring criminal thread, all Pokemon through Generation 9 are obtainable before the final boss, and every region's original storyline is preserved while being pulled into a new meta-plot about forced unity, legendary energy, and the danger of turning the Pokemon world into one controlled system.

Proposed creative identity: this should feel like "Pokemon Red becomes the entire series." Kanto is the emotional home, but every later region teaches the player a new idea about Pokemon: tradition, ecology, myth, freedom, beauty, family, spectacle, and purpose.

## 2. Meta-Narrative Arc

### Core Premise

Professor Oak launches the World Pokedex Initiative after Pallet Town reports impossible early-route migration: Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, Kalos, Alola, Galar, and Paldea starters appear on Routes 1-3. Oak believes this is a once-in-a-century ecological event. Giovanni knows it is not. A hidden organization called the Nexus Order is using stolen regional research, legendary artifacts, and criminal networks to synchronize the world's energy lines.

The Nexus Order's machine is the Meridian Engine. It is designed to pull every region into one controllable "perfect continent" called the Nexus Dominion. It does not merge land cleanly. It bends weather, migration, time, dreams, oceans, space-time, Ultra Wormholes, Dynamax energy, and Tera crystals until the borders between regions break.

### Why Antman Travels All Regions

Antman begins as a new Pallet trainer walking the same road Red once walked. Red is nearby at the start as a friend, mentor, and occasional travel companion, but he does not replace Antman's agency. Oak's WorldLink device marks Antman as the field researcher consistently present at each anomaly. After Kanto, the Pokemon League creates the World Circuit: a legal trainer route between regions that lets champions, researchers, and elite challengers investigate anomalies while earning regional badges/trials. Antman is invited because he defeats Team Rocket in Kanto and because his Pokedex records the earliest cross-region species shift.

### The Villain Web

Team Rocket is the spine. Rocket operates in every region because its business model scales: Pokemon trafficking, rare items, casino money, Silph technology, black-market TMs, and corrupt transport routes. Giovanni wants a world he can profit from, not a broken world.

Nexus Order manipulates existing villain teams by giving each one exactly what it wants:

- Rocket receives inter-region smuggling tech.
- Magma receives geothermal amplifiers.
- Aqua receives ocean-current destabilizers.
- Galactic receives space-time calculation cores.
- Plasma receives propaganda tools and liberation data.
- Flare receives old-war energy conduits.
- Aether receives wormhole stabilization data.
- Macro Cosmos receives energy forecasts.
- Phoenix receives fossil resurrection matrices.
- Moonlight receives dream-frequency transmitters.
- Gold Dust receives monopoly access to rare minerals, shinies, and battle economy resources.

Each team thinks it can betray the others first. The Nexus Order is counting on that.

### Region Order Justification

The order follows mainline history and rising cosmic stakes:

1. Kanto teaches the basics and introduces Rocket.
2. Johto reveals old legends and Rocket's persistence.
3. Hoenn proves nature can be destabilized at regional scale.
4. Sinnoh/Hisui reveals the ancient space-time foundation.
5. Unova turns the conflict ideological and public.
6. Kalos shows ancient weaponized energy.
7. Alola introduces interdimensional instability.
8. Galar introduces mass energy infrastructure and stadium-scale spectacle.
9. Paldea reveals the final piece: Tera energy and Area Zero as a natural mirror of the artificial Nexus.

Proposed: keep this exact order. It honors the series chronology, gives clean mechanical escalation, and lets Red's simple Kanto journey grow into the entire Pokemon world.

## 3. Per-Region Breakdown

### Kanto - The First Distortion

Tone: classic, bright, direct, mysterious under the surface.
Original story preserved: Pallet start, eight badges, Team Rocket, Silph Co., Pokemon Tower, Cinnabar, Indigo Plateau.
Twist: cross-region starters on Routes 1-3 are evidence of a Meridian leak.
Villains: Team Rocket, early Moonlight hints, early Gold Dust buyers, Phoenix fossil foreshadowing.
Key characters: Oak, Red, Blue, Brock, Misty, Lt. Surge, Erika, Koga, Sabrina, Blaine, Giovanni, Lorelei, Bruno, Agatha, Lance.
Signature mechanic showcased: starter choice, WorldLink rival feed, level caps, early rare encounters.

Plot beats:

- Oak offers a 39-choice starter program: 27 official starters plus 12 special candidates.
- Blue, Ava, and Dax start at the same time as Antman.
- Red appears as Antman's early friend/travel companion and notices the route migration before most adults do.
- Viridian and Mt. Moon establish Rocket as more active than in vanilla Red.
- Lavender Tower introduces dream static from Team Moonlight.
- Celadon/Saffron show Rocket moving Silph tech into global trade routes.
- Cinnabar's fossil lab reveals Team Phoenix has bought old resurrection data.
- Giovanni vanishes after discovering a "Nexus sponsor" is using Rocket.

Gym/league structure: classic eight Kanto gyms plus Indigo League.
Exit: after becoming Kanto Champion or clearing the Rocket/Silph climax, Antman receives a World Circuit Passport and travels to Johto by Magnet Train/ferry.

### Johto - History Sends a Warning

Tone: traditional, haunted, mythic, autumnal.
Original story preserved: New Bark/Elm thread, Sprout Tower, Burned Tower, Lake of Rage, Radio Tower, Johto League.
Twist: Johto's towers are ancient regulators that once kept regional energy lines separate.
Villains: Rocket remnants, Moonlight, early Nexus agents.
Key characters: Professor Elm, Silver, Eusine, Morty, Jasmine, Clair, Lance, Kimono Girls.
Signature mechanic showcased: day/night, friendship, roaming legends, rematches.

Plot beats:

- Silver joins the rival network while hunting Rocket.
- Rocket uses Goldenrod Radio Tower to send a signal to Giovanni and accidentally amplifies Nexus frequencies.
- Moonlight traps Ecruteak citizens in dream loops near the Burned Tower.
- Ho-Oh and Lugia react to the Meridian Engine as if an old disaster is repeating.
- Lance confirms Rocket is no longer just a Kanto problem.

Gym/league structure: eight Johto gyms; Elite Four rematch can use combined Kanto/Johto scaling.
Exit: clearing Radio Tower and Johto League unlocks global ferry routes to Hoenn.

### Hoenn - Land, Sea, and Climate Collapse

Tone: tropical, adventurous, weather-driven, ecological.
Original story preserved: Magma vs. Aqua, Devon goods, Meteor Falls, weather crisis, Groudon/Kyogre/Rayquaza.
Twist: Magma and Aqua's plans are made worse by foreign tech they do not fully understand.
Villains: Magma, Aqua, Rocket smugglers, Gold Dust mineral buyers.
Key characters: Birch, Brendan/May, Steven, Wallace, Maxie, Archie, Zinnia.
Signature mechanic showcased: weather encounter tables, diving, secret bases, expanded fishing.

Plot beats:

- Brendan/May becomes a field-rival who shares weather data through WorldLink.
- Gold Dust pays for meteorite fragments and rare stones.
- Magma and Aqua each believe Nexus tech will make their side unstoppable.
- The weather crisis spreads beyond Hoenn, affecting Kanto and Johto route tables.
- Rayquaza interrupts the disaster but marks Antman's WorldLink with a sky glyph that later unlocks anomaly tracking.

Gym/league structure: eight Hoenn gyms; Wallace/Steven championship branch.
Exit: Devon and Steven arrange transit to Sinnoh after detecting space-time distortion in the Rayquaza data.

### Sinnoh / Hisui - The World Has a Memory

Tone: cold, ancient, religious, dangerous.
Original story preserved: Galactic, Mt. Coronet, lake guardians, Dialga/Palkia, Giratina/Distortion World.
Twist: Hisui flashbacks show that forced regional convergence happened once in prehistory and nearly erased natural Pokemon migration.
Villains: Team Galactic, Phoenix, Moonlight.
Key characters: Rowan, Dawn/Lucas, Cynthia, Cyrus, Looker, Volo echoes, Adaman/Irida legacy references.
Signature mechanic showcased: ancient forms, lore quests, space-time anomaly dungeons.

Plot beats:

- Cynthia becomes the main lore mentor and optional superboss.
- Dawn/Lucas joins as a research rival focused on ruins and legendary history.
- Phoenix revives unstable ancient forms from Hisui records.
- Galactic's space-time research becomes the blueprint Nexus Order needs.
- Giratina shows that the Meridian Engine is tearing a false corridor through reality.

Gym/league structure: eight Sinnoh gyms plus Distortion World climax.
Exit: Looker traces Nexus funding to Castelia's black market in Unova.

### Unova - Truth, Ideals, and Public War

Tone: metropolitan, ideological, political, fast-moving.
Original story preserved: Plasma liberation debate, N, Gym circuit, legendary dragon conflict.
Twist: Nexus Order uses Plasma's language of liberation to justify central control of all Pokemon movement.
Villains: Team Plasma split, Rocket finance arm, Gold Dust.
Key characters: Juniper, N, Cheren, Bianca, Alder, Iris, Ghetsis.
Signature mechanic showcased: triple/rotation/tag battle variants, public reputation, battle subway.

Plot beats:

- N challenges Antman morally: is catching all Pokemon research, collection, or control?
- Plasma splits into true liberation allies and Ghetsis loyalists.
- Rocket launders money through Castelia imports and rare Pokemon auctions.
- Gold Dust manipulates rare-item prices and shiny rumors.
- Reshiram/Zekrom event asks the player to choose Truth, Ideals, or Balance; the choice changes dialogue and final boss support, not access to completion.

Gym/league structure: eight Unova gyms; Plasma Castle interrupts League.
Exit: AZ's name appears in recovered energy documents, sending the party to Kalos.

### Kalos - Beauty Built on a Weapon

Tone: elegant, stylish, romantic, hiding old horror.
Original story preserved: Mega Evolution, Team Flare, AZ, ultimate weapon.
Twist: the Kalos weapon is one of the first human-made attempts to force world energy into a single channel.
Villains: Team Flare, Gold Dust, Nexus Order observers.
Key characters: Sycamore, Serena/Calem, Lysandre, AZ, Diantha, Korrina.
Signature mechanic showcased: Mega Evolution, customization, boutique systems.

Plot beats:

- Serena/Calem joins as a rival focused on style, Mega Evolution, and public image.
- Gold Dust funds Flare by buying exclusive access to Mega Stones and rare minerals.
- Lysandre sees Nexus Order as proof that only selected people should survive the future.
- AZ recognizes the Meridian Engine's energy signature as a descendant of the ultimate weapon.
- Antman receives the Keystone Resonator, letting Mega Evolution become a controlled world mechanic.

Gym/league structure: eight Kalos gyms; Flare weapon climax before League completion.
Exit: Sycamore and AZ detect the same energy inside Ultra Wormhole readings from Alola.

### Alola - Family, Islands, and Ultra Breaches

Tone: warm, spiritual, strange, personal.
Original story preserved: island challenge, Team Skull, Aether, Lusamine, Ultra Beasts.
Twist: Ultra Space is the one force Nexus Order cannot fully predict; it can break the Meridian Engine or supercharge it.
Villains: Aether corruption, Team Skull at first, Nexus Order wormhole unit.
Key characters: Kukui, Lillie, Gladion, Hau, Guzma, Lusamine, Kahunas, Trial Captains.
Signature mechanic showcased: Z-Moves, regional variants, trials instead of gyms, Ultra Beast hunts.

Plot beats:

- Alola pauses the badge formula and reframes progress through trials.
- Team Skull begins as a nuisance but becomes an ally network against Aether and Nexus.
- Lillie's family arc becomes the emotional center.
- Ultra Beasts appear as boss encounters and later postgame hunts.
- Solgaleo/Lunala can open safe transit corridors, proving not all dimensional travel is destructive.

Gym/league structure: island trials, kahunas, Alola League.
Exit: Aether data shows Macro Cosmos has been buying energy-stabilization tech in Galar.

### Galar - Spectacle Hides the Machine

Tone: sports drama, industrial, public, competitive.
Original story preserved: gym stadiums, rivals, Macro Cosmos, Rose, Eternatus, Champion Cup.
Twist: Galar's league infrastructure is the first system large enough to stabilize Nexus energy across a whole region.
Villains: Macro Cosmos, Rocket betting ring, Gold Dust raid monopoly.
Key characters: Leon, Hop, Marnie, Bede, Sonia, Rose, Oleana, Mustard.
Signature mechanic showcased: Dynamax/Gigantamax, raids, stadium boss design.

Plot beats:

- Hop, Marnie, and Bede rotate as rival bosses; Marnie is the most useful ally.
- Rocket runs illegal battle betting under the stadium circuit.
- Gold Dust tries to monopolize raid drops, Bottle Caps, and rare fossils.
- Rose believes the Meridian Engine could solve the energy crisis if controlled by the right hands.
- Eternatus energy is stolen during the climax instead of fully contained.

Gym/league structure: stadium gyms, Champion Cup, restricted Dynamax only in power spots.
Exit: Sonia traces stolen energy shipments to Paldea's Area Zero research network.

### Paldea - Treasure, Academy, and the Final Secret

Tone: open, exploratory, academic, final-revelation.
Original story preserved: academy, Victory Road, Path of Legends, Starfall Street, Area Zero, Tera.
Twist: Area Zero is not the cause of the Nexus crisis. It is the natural phenomenon the Meridian Engine is imitating.
Villains: misunderstood Team Star, Phoenix, final Nexus Order, Rocket schism.
Key characters: Nemona, Penny, Arven, Clavell, Geeta, Sada/Turo-inspired AI, Team Star bosses.
Signature mechanic showcased: Terastallization, open objective routing, final hub systems.

Plot beats:

- Mesagoza Academy becomes the global research hub for the final act.
- Nemona becomes the strongest friendly rival and tests whether Antman still loves battling after saving the world repeatedly.
- Arven's Titan quest ties Herba Mystica to ecological repair.
- Penny exposes Nexus Order's communications by using Team Star's underground network.
- Phoenix attempts to revive extinct/paradox life as controllable weapons.
- Area Zero reveals the Meridian Engine is a crude artificial copy of natural convergence.

Gym/league structure: Paldea's three-path structure compressed into a chapter: gyms, Titans, Team Star, then Area Zero.
Exit: Paldea opens the World Nexus Championship and final dungeon.

## 4. Rival System Design

### Design Goal

Rivals should feel like active trainers, not checkpoints. They should move through the same world, win and lose badges, catch Pokemon, send notifications, ask for tag battles, and appear in towns depending on story state.

Recommended implementation: hybrid system. Major rival progress is scripted by player story flags. Minor captures, losses, training, and city sightings are simulated through controlled event tables.

### The 10 Rivals

1. Blue Oak
   - Starter: counter-pick from Kanto starters by default; can use a regional counter if player picks a non-Kanto starter.
   - Personality: arrogant, gifted, insecure under pressure.
   - Team archetype: balanced competitive coverage.
   - Narrative role: the classic mirror. He wants to beat Antman before understanding the world is bigger than Kanto.
   - Intersections: Pallet, Cerulean, S.S. Anne, Pokemon Tower, Silph, Indigo, World Championship.

2. Ava Pine
   - Starter: Chikorita, Popplio, or Sprigatito preference; final choice selected to avoid duplicating player.
   - Personality: friendly researcher, brave when others hesitate.
   - Team archetype: support, status, bulky pivots, rare captures.
   - Narrative role: Pokedex conscience. She asks whether documenting every Pokemon means protecting them.
   - Intersections: early Kanto, Johto towers, Hoenn weather labs, Sinnoh ruins, Paldea academy.

3. Dax Vale
   - Starter: Cyndaquil, Torchic, Chimchar, Scorbunny, or Fuecoco preference.
   - Personality: aggressive speedrunner, loves hard battles, hates excuses.
   - Team archetype: offense, hazards, priority, weather abuse.
   - Narrative role: pressure rival. He forces the player to respect level caps and team building.
   - Intersections: Brock race, Surge battle prep, Hoenn Magma/Aqua fights, Galar stadiums.

4. Silver
   - Starter: Totodile or Sneasel-centered Johto team depending on engine support.
   - Personality: harsh, wounded, anti-Rocket.
   - Team archetype: dark, poison, fast physical attackers evolving into disciplined balance.
   - Narrative role: Rocket trauma and redemption.
   - Intersections: Johto, Goldenrod, Mahogany, Rocket raids, final anti-Rocket tag battle.

5. May or Brendan
   - Starter: one Hoenn starter not used by player/rivals.
   - Personality: field researcher, practical, upbeat.
   - Team archetype: terrain/weather balance, regional ecology.
   - Narrative role: weather-system guide.
   - Intersections: Hoenn routes, Weather Institute, Rayquaza climax.

6. Dawn or Lucas
   - Starter: Sinnoh starter or Hisui-form anchor.
   - Personality: patient, scholarly, myth-focused.
   - Team archetype: setup balance, psychic/steel/dragon lore picks.
   - Narrative role: ruins and space-time researcher.
   - Intersections: Sinnoh ruins, lakes, Mt. Coronet, Hisui flashbacks.

7. N / Unova Idealist Slot
   - Starter: none fixed; rotating team of Pokemon he has befriended.
   - Personality: compassionate, intense, philosophical.
   - Team archetype: natural teams from local areas, legendary-adjacent late game.
   - Narrative role: ethical challenge to "catch them all."
   - Intersections: Unova story, Plasma split, final world choice.

8. Serena or Calem
   - Starter: Kalos starter.
   - Personality: stylish, composed, ambitious.
   - Team archetype: Mega Evolution ace plus elegant coverage.
   - Narrative role: public image and Mega mastery.
   - Intersections: Kalos routes, Tower of Mastery, Flare climax, championship.

9. Marnie / Hop / Bede Galar Slot
   - Proposed default: Marnie as primary, Hop and Bede as recurring bosses.
   - Starter: Morpeko anchor for Marnie; Hop uses Galar starter; Bede uses psychic/fairy.
   - Personality: grounded, dry, loyal.
   - Team archetype: dark/fairy pressure, anti-meta.
   - Narrative role: cuts through stadium spectacle and exposes corruption.
   - Intersections: Galar gyms, Spikemuth, Rose Tower, Champion Cup.

10. Nemona / Penny / Arven Paldea Slot
   - Proposed default: Nemona as main rival, Penny and Arven as story allies.
   - Starter: Paldea starter not selected; late-game ace built for Tera.
   - Personality: battle genius, joyful, relentless.
   - Team archetype: high-skill offensive balance, Tera mind games.
   - Narrative role: final friendly rival who reminds the player that the journey is still supposed to be fun.
   - Intersections: Mesagoza, gyms, Area Zero, World Championship final ladder.

Frequency:

- Major story: immediate.
- Minor captures: batched when entering a town or opening WorldLink.
- Rival progress feed: always accessible.
- Settings: All, Major Only, Rivals Only, Off.

## 5. Antagonist Organizations

### Team Rocket

Leader: Giovanni.
Goal: profit, control, technology, rare Pokemon ownership.
Methods: theft, casinos, black market, Silph tech, smuggling, bribery.
Signature Pokemon: Persian, Nidoking, Nidoqueen, Rhydon/Rhyperior, Kangaskhan, Mewtwo-related shadows if used carefully.
Arc: starts as the main Kanto enemy, becomes the recurring criminal network, then splits when Giovanni realizes Nexus Order would destroy the market he wants to rule.

Leader: Maxie.
Goal: expand land and human development.
Methods: geothermal drilling, volcano control, Groudon awakening.
Signature Pokemon: Camerupt, Mightyena, Crobat, Groudon-linked bosses.
Arc: manipulated with Nexus geothermal amplifiers.

### Team Aqua

Leader: Archie.
Goal: expand seas and restore nature.
Methods: ocean-current disruption, Kyogre awakening, naval raids.
Signature Pokemon: Sharpedo, Crawdaunt, Walrein, Kyogre-linked bosses.
Arc: manipulated with Nexus current stabilizers.

### Team Galactic

Leader: Cyrus.
Goal: remake reality without spirit.
Methods: space-time machinery, lake guardian extraction.
Signature Pokemon: Weavile, Honchkrow, Crobat, Magnezone, Dialga/Palkia pressure.

Leaders: N and Ghetsis split.
Goal: liberation for true believers; domination for Ghetsis.

### Aether Foundation / Team Skull

Goal: Aether seeks control through research; Skull seeks belonging and recognition.

Leader: Rose.
Goal: solve future energy crisis at any cost.
Methods: corporate infrastructure, stadium power systems, Eternatus research.
Signature Pokemon: Copperajah, Ferrothorn, Escavalier, Eternatus event.
Arc: gives Nexus Order industrial scale.

### Team Star

Leader network: Penny and squad bosses.
Goal: self-defense, identity, survival after being misunderstood.
Methods: underground communications, mobile bases, academy resistance.
Signature Pokemon: Revavroom, squad type aces.
Arc: starts as apparent antagonist, becomes the information network that exposes Nexus Order.

### Team Phoenix

Leader: Dr. Solara Venn.
Goal: revive, clone, and "improve" extinct or lost Pokemon to overcome natural limits.
Methods: fossil labs, ancient DNA, unstable evolution catalysts, artificial legendary research.
Signature Pokemon: Armarouge, Talonflame, Volcarona, Aerodactyl, Hisuian forms, revived fossils.
Arc: begins at Cinnabar, grows in Sinnoh/Hisui, peaks in Paldea with paradox/fossil weapons.

### Team Moonlight

Leader: Nocturne Vale.
Goal: control memory, dreams, fear, and belief so people accept forced unity peacefully.
Methods: dream waves, lunar rituals, illusion fields, Darkrai/Cresselia mythology.
Signature Pokemon: Darkrai, Cresselia-adjacent encounters, Lunatone, Umbreon, Mismagius, Hatterene.
Arc: starts in Lavender, deepens in Johto/Sinnoh, attempts to pacify crowds during the World Championship.

### Team Gold Dust

Leader: Aurelia Midas.
Goal: own scarcity itself: rare Pokemon, shiny Pokemon, Tera shards, Mega Stones, Bottle Caps, fossils, mints, and competitive items.
Methods: auctions, mining, market manipulation, paid trainers, item monopolies.
Signature Pokemon: Persian, Gholdengo, Sableye, Carbink/Diancie event, Kingambit, shiny-themed boss teams.
Arc: finances other villains, then betrays them when Nexus threatens to make rarity meaningless.

### Nexus Order

Leader: Director Caelum Voss.
Public face: a philanthropic world-unity foundation funding research and transportation.
True goal: activate the Meridian Engine and create the Nexus Dominion, a single controllable world system.
Methods: sponsorship, infiltration, stolen legendary data, manipulated villain teams.
Signature Pokemon: Porygon-Z, Metagross, Rotom, Type: Null/Silvally-style engineered theme, Terapagos-linked final energy, multi-gimmick champion team.
Arc: invisible until Sinnoh, named in Unova/Kalos, openly active in Paldea, final enemy in World Nexus Championship.

## 6. Systems Spec

### Starter and Early Route System

Starter lab offers 39 choices:

- 27 official starters from Kanto through Paldea.
- 12 special starters: Eevee, Pikachu, Dratini, Abra, Gastly, Larvitar, Sandile, Kubfu, Staryu, Shroomish, Rockruff, Ralts.

Routes 1-3 include every starter and special starter as rare encounters. Proposed balancing:

- Safe starters: 1-3 percent by route/time segment.
- Eevee, Pikachu, Abra, Gastly, Staryu, Shroomish, Rockruff, Ralts: 2-5 percent.
- Dratini, Larvitar: 1 percent, low level, rare weather/time condition.
- Kubfu: not a normal grass encounter. It should be a one-time Route 3 monastery event after first badge or an ultra-rare WorldLink anomaly. This preserves excitement without breaking the early game.

### Battle Mechanics

Required baseline:

- Physical/Special split.
- Fairy type.
- Modern move categories.
- Modern abilities and moves through Gen 9 where engine support exists.
- Expanded reusable TM list.
- Ability Capsule and Ability Patch.
- Held items.
- Better AI profiles.
- Optional EXP Share behavior.

Proposed gimmick gating:

- Mega Evolution: preview in Kanto postgame rematch, full unlock in Kalos.
- Z-Moves: Alola only until postgame unless a special facility unlock is added.
- Dynamax/Gigantamax: no early-game use; controlled usability can unlock after Hoenn, with Galar remaining the full story home.
- Terastallization: no early-game use; controlled usability can unlock after Hoenn, with Paldea remaining the full story home.

Reason: allowing every gimmick everywhere from the start destroys balance and makes bosses incoherent. After the third region, controlled access can work because the player has enough team depth and game knowledge.

### QoL and Progression

- Infinite Repel: toggle from bag/WorldLink; blocks normal wild battles, not static encounters, overworld encounters, shinies, or story events.
- Portable PC: WorldLink Box Sync unlocked after Pewter; full portable PC after Kanto third badge.
- Portable Healing: limited-use Field Medic early; full portable healing only on Casual/Standard or as late-game item. Hardcore/Nuzlocke disables or restricts it.
- Skip text/fast-forward: options menu toggles. Battle animation skip should be separate from text speed.
- Infinite Rare Candies: sold after first badge in Modern/Challenge modes. Level caps prevent abuse.
- Trainer rematches: VS Seeker-style item plus Pokecenter rematch board.
- Adjusted evolutions: trade evolutions use Link Cable item or level/item alternatives.

### Level Caps and Difficulty

Modes:

- Casual: soft caps, portable healing allowed, lower AI.
- Standard: soft caps, normal items, smart bosses.
- Hard: hard caps, stronger AI, better held items.
- Expert: hard caps, limited bag items, competitive teams.
- Nuzlocke: hard caps, first encounter rules, permadeath box, optional item bans.
- Custom: player toggles each rule.

Tradeoff: Rare Candies plus level caps is good design. It removes boring grinding while preserving team-building pressure.

### Fishing

Four rods:

- Old Rod: common water Pokemon.
- Good Rod: mid-tier and regional fish.

## 9. Technical Foundation Notes

### Recommendation

Use pokeemerald-expansion as the primary technical base because the target is a `.gba` playable in OpenEmu. It is the most realistic GBA path for modern battle mechanics, Gen 9-style data, Fairy type, level caps, follower support, day/night, expanded moves/abilities/items, and decomp-friendly development.

Do not use Radical Red as a base unless explicit permission and source availability exist. Treat Radical Red as design inspiration, not a technical foundation.

### Alternative

If the non-negotiable goal changes from "playable `.gba` in OpenEmu" to "maximum content with fewer hardware limits," Pokemon Essentials/RPG Maker XP or another PC fangame stack would be more realistic. For this project, the GBA target wins, so each region must be a full-feeling 10+ hour curated chapter rather than a tile-for-tile copy of nine retail games.

### Integrate vs. Build

Use existing/open-source engine features where available:

- Physical/Special split.
- Fairy type.
- Gen 9 species/moves/items/abilities.
- Level caps.
- AI improvements.
- Follower Pokemon support.
- Day/night/weather.
- Debug tools.
- Dex expansion.

Build project-specific systems:

- 39-choice starter UI.
- WorldLink rival feed.
- 10-rival state system.
- Meridian/Nexus story flags.
- Region unlock/transport hub.
- Expanded Pokecenter services.
- Multi-region mart economy.
- Cross-region encounter policy.
- Team Phoenix, Moonlight, Gold Dust, Nexus Order events.

### Scope Risks

Realistic for a phased ROM hack:

- Kanto vertical slice.
- all-starter selection.
- early rare encounters.
- three starting rivals.
- WorldLink prototype.
- modern battle mechanics.
- expanded marts/Pokecenters.
- level caps/Nuzlocke tools.

Stretch:

- all nine regions in one GBA ROM.
- all Pokemon through Gen 9 with forms, sprites, cries, learnsets, abilities.
- following sprites for all species.
- overworld Pokemon everywhere.
- all battle gimmicks fully active.
- dynamic rival simulation.
- custom soundtrack across nine regions.

Recommended scope rule: ship chapters. Every region must be done, but never build all nine regions before Kanto is fun.

### Distribution Rule

Do not distribute ROM files. Build source privately and release legal patch files only. Do not include copyrighted ROMs in repository. Credit every engine, art, music, and community resource.
