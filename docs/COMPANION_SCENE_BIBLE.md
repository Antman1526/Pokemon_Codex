# Pokemon Nexus Red - Companion Scene Bible

Date: 2026-06-13
Focus characters: Red, Brock, Misty, May

## 1. Companion Philosophy

Pokemon Nexus Red should feel like a long journey with friends, not a lonely badge checklist. Companions should add warmth, tactical advice, optional training, and tag battle spectacle while keeping Antman as the person who earns badges, clears major trials, and makes the final choices.

Rules:

- Companions may follow Antman during routes, towns, dungeons, and side quests.
- Companions may join tag battles, training battles, and villain skirmishes.
- Companions may give advice before gyms.
- Companions do not fight Antman's gym battles.
- Companions do not solve major solo trials.
- Companion dialogue should be short, useful, and characterful.

## 2. Red

Role: full-game travel companion, mentor-friend, original-road anchor.

Red is present across the entire game. He is quiet but not absent. He should talk enough that the player feels friendship, but not so much that he loses his identity.

Scene types:

- route observations,
- training advice,
- tag battles,
- quiet emotional scenes,
- WorldLink notes,
- postgame friend battle.

Key scenes:

- Pallet: Red notices strange tracks.
- Route 1: Red follows briefly and points out impossible migration.
- Pewter Museum: Red can be backup tag partner.
- Lavender Tower: Red helps protect Antman from a dream disturbance.
- Cinnabar: Red talks about power and restraint.
- Johto: Red appears near Mt. Silver/Rocket echoes.
- Hoenn: Red helps during a dangerous weather route.
- Sinnoh: Red trains Antman before Mt. Coronet.
- Unova: Red silently contrasts N's ideology.
- Kalos: Red questions using power for image.
- Alola: Red helps during an Ultra Beast civilian rescue.
- Galar: Red joins an exhibition/training scene.
- Paldea: Red follows Antman's lead at Area Zero's threshold.
- World Nexus Championship: Red tag battle plus postgame friend battle.

## 3. Brock

Role: early mentor, survival expert, training friend.

Brock becomes a friend after the Boulder Badge. He should feel practical, warm, and useful. His scenes should teach field preparation, team roles, and caring for Pokemon over a very long journey.

Scene types:

- post-gym respect scene,
- training camp,
- cooking/survival scene,
- Rock/Ground counterplay lesson,
- breeder/daycare advice,
- tag battle against Rocket,
- rematch mentor.

Key scenes:

- Pewter post-gym: Brock acknowledges Antman's unusual starter path and gives field advice.
- Mt. Moon entrance: Brock appears if the player has trouble or returns to investigate Rocket fossil theft.
- Cerulean route camp: Brock explains long-route preparation and status recovery.
- Rock Tunnel: Brock teaches cave navigation and the value of non-damage utility.
- Cinnabar: Brock comments on fossil revival ethics and Phoenix research.
- World Championship: Brock runs a preparation room for long tournament battles.

Brock should not follow constantly, but he should become the first adult-ish friend who checks in over the long journey.

## 4. Misty

Role: confidence friend, water-route expert, tempo teacher.

Misty becomes a recurring friend after Cerulean. She should be energetic, direct, and competitive. Her scenes should teach speed control, water traversal, and emotional momentum after losses.

Scene types:

- post-gym respect scene,
- water route escort,
- training swim,
- tag battle against Rocket/Aqua,
- confidence scene after a rival setback,
- rematch mentor.

Key scenes:

- Cerulean post-gym: Misty respects Antman's adaptability and teases Blue/Dax.
- Route to Vermilion: Misty appears near water routes and gives Old Rod/Fishing advice if not already obtained.
- S.S. Anne: Misty recognizes suspicious cargo routes and helps decode waterway manifests.
- Fuchsia/Safari: Misty helps identify non-native water Pokemon in Safari anomaly pools.
- Hoenn: Misty appears during the Aqua/Kyogre arc as a visiting water specialist.
- World Championship: Misty offers a high-tempo rematch and doubles prep.

Misty should be a friend who makes the journey feel fun and competitive, not just a gym leader who disappears.

## 5. May

Role: Hoenn field companion, ecology friend, weather researcher.

May becomes a major friend in Hoenn. She should carry the sense of tropical field adventure and help the player understand weather, habitats, and ecological stakes.

Scene types:

- field research route,
- weather data collection,
- tag battle against Magma/Aqua,
- rare Pokemon habitat scene,
- emotional ecology scene after weather crisis,
- WorldLink research updates.

Key scenes:

- Hoenn arrival: May introduces the local field research rhythm.
- Weather Institute: May becomes an active tag partner.
- Mt. Chimney: May reacts to Magma's land-extremism.
- Route 119: May helps track weather-dependent Pokemon.
- Sootopolis: May witnesses the land/sea crisis and helps with evacuation.
- Sky Pillar aftermath: May helps interpret Rayquaza's effect on WorldLink anomaly tracking.
- World Championship: May joins a research-team support scene with Ava.

May should make Hoenn feel like an expedition, not only a badge path.

## 6. Companion Balance

The player should never feel overcrowded. Use rotating companion presence:

- Red: most consistent emotional companion.
- Brock: practical mentor scenes.
- Misty: energetic battle/water-route friend.
- May: Hoenn ecology/travel partner.

When multiple companions are present, give each a clear role:

- Red notices danger.
- Brock prepares the team.
- Misty pushes action.
- May interprets ecology.

## 7. Companion Rewards

Companion scenes can reward:

- held items,
- berries,
- fishing upgrades,
- field utility hints,
- training rematches,
- WorldLink checklist hints,
- rare encounter tips,
- double battle practice,
- friendship-based side quests.

Avoid over-rewarding every scene. Sometimes the reward is character, worldbuilding, or a new objective.
